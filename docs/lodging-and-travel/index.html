Travel

flying

The closest major airport to Seattle is SeaTac (SEA). From there, you can get up to Seattle by rental car or Lyft/Uber (~30 minutes), or reserve a spot on a shuttle.

It's also easy, though a bit slow, to take the lightrail: From baggage claim, follow signs for the Link Light Rail (about a 10 minute walk, past the parking garage). Get a single-ride ticket (or ORCA card) from the ticket machines before going up to the train platform. You don't have to give anyone the ticket to get on the train, but hold on to it until you disembark, in case the train cops board to check.

public transit

OneBusAway is a pretty good local transit app that shows bus and lightrail schedules and which lines come to a given stop.

Bus fare is $2.75, cash only, no change available. Lightrail fare varies, and you have to get at ticket at the machines at the stations before getting on the train.

Alternatively, get an ORCA card at the airport lightrail station and load it up. This works for both lightrail and buses.

walk or bike

Seattle is very walkable and bikeable. The two bike-share programs currently operating are Lime and Jump. (Read about the demise of at least two previous programs here.) These both include some electric bikes, which are awesome for hills and make you feel like a magical beast. BYO-helmet. Ride safe.
