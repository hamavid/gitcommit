Lodging

stay at a hotel or b&b

These suggestions are a hodge podge from The Abbey and some semi-educated Googling. We cannot vouch for the accuracy or quality of all of them, but hopefully they are a good start.

  • Chelsea Station Inn - a quaint B&B just up the street from the Abbey.
  • Art Hostel - A cool hostel in Fremont, near the Abbey.
  • City Hostel - Arty hostel in Belltown, shared bathrooms and free breakfast.
  • Shafer Baillie Mansion B&B - A B&B on Capitol Hill near Volunteer Park.
  • Hotel Ballard - Newly renovated boutique in Ballard with amazing rooftop rooms and garden.
  • The Maxwell - Hotel at The Seattle Center.
  • Courtyard and Residence Inn by Marriott- Hotels in South Lake Union near the MOHAI.
  • Graduate - Art deco hotel in the University District.
  • Marco Polo Motel - A basic motel very near the venue.
  • The College Inn - Hotel in the Udistrict with shared bathrooms, free breakfast, and a pretty rowdy bar in the basement in case you want to keep the party going.

Travel

flying

The closest major airport to Seattle is SeaTac (SEA). From there, you can get up to Seattle by rental car or Lyft/Uber (~30 minutes), or reserve a spot on a shuttle.

You can also take the lightrail: From baggage claim, follow signs for the Link Light Rail (about a 10 minute walk, past the parking garage). Use the ticket machines to buy a single-ride ticket, or get an ORCA card and tap it, then go up to the train platform. You don't have to give anyone the ticket to get on the train, but hold on to it until you disembark, in case the train cops board to check.

public transit

Bus fare is $2.75, cash only, no change available. Lightrail fare varies, and you have to get a ticket at the machines at the stations before boarding. You can also use the Transit Go app to pay with your phone. Alternatively, get an ORCA card for $5 and add E-purse value. ORCA cards work for both lightrail and buses and include transfers between them.

OneBusAway is a local transit app that shows bus and lightrail schedules and which lines come to a given stop.

Unfortunately, 10/26-10/27 is one of the weekends with major construction planned on the lightrail this fall, so allow extra time for lightrailing. There will be shuttles through downtown, everything will be crowded, and you can't bring that bicycle you had on the plane. We forgot to tell city council about our wedding, sorry.

walk or bike

Seattle is very walkable and bikeable. The two bike-share programs currently operating are Lime and Jump. (Read about the demise of at least two previous programs here.) These both include some electric bikes, which are helpful for hills and make you feel like a magical beast. BYO-helmet. Ride safe.
